---
title: Scheduling & Slots
subtitle: How posts occupy time slots, and why you sometimes get a 409.
---

Tempo publishes on discrete **time slots** derived from a channel's weekly schedule
(`GET /schedule`). Understanding slots explains most of the API's write behavior.

## Auto-slotting vs. pinned times

- **Auto-slotting** — create a post without `scheduled_at` and it is placed in the next
  open slot, ordered by `sort_order`.
- **Pinned** — pass a `scheduled_at` and the post is locked to that exact slot.

## Slot conflicts return 409

A database-level uniqueness guarantee allows only one active post per slot. If you pin a
post to a slot that's already taken, or a reorder would collide, the API responds with:

```json
{ "error": "That time slot is already taken." }
```

with status `409 Conflict`. Handle it by choosing another time or letting Tempo
auto-slot the post.

## Category-pinned posts

Posts created from an automation rule are tied to their category's slot and are excluded
from manual reordering, so automated cadence stays intact.

<Tip>
When you save a full schedule with `POST /schedule`, Tempo re-slots existing manual,
uncategorized posts to fit the new windows. A conflict during that re-slot also surfaces
as `409`.
</Tip>
